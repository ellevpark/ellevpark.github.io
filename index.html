A little more about me

I came to engineering through a non-traditional path. I studied Psychology and Art History at Boston University and began my career at a nonprofit focused on affordable and fair housing in New York. That experience shaped how I think about access, equity, and the real-world impact of thoughtful systems.

As a full-stack engineer, I am interested in the small product decisions that make experiences feel clearer, easier, and more trustworthy. I am drawn to the space between users, design, and engineering, where thoughtful ideas become useful products. I redesigned an Angular registration flow after noticing a pattern of onboarding support tickets. I rebuilt it to be more intuitive, with real-time validation so people could catch errors early. Small decisions like that reduce friction and build trust.

That's why I want to move into product management. I like ambiguous problems, weighing the product and design decisions that shape how something feels to use, and asking whether a solution truly helps the people it's for.
